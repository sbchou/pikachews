When I was growing up, for some reason or the other (creativity? laughs? the unbridled joy of childhood?), my sister and her friend used to always say "Swiss Chard and Rutebega" in a ridiculous, prentitious, quasi-British accent. I never figured out why it was these vegetables that were the source of many giggles, aside from their funny names, and for nearly two decades, I had never tasted either.

All that changed yesterday while waiting in the ridiculously long (seriously, comical) line at Trader Joe's. A bag of Kalediscope Chard caught my eye-- with those pretty colors and fresh green leaves, how could I say no?

I've never cooked chard before but I figured like all dark leafy greens sauteeing is the best way to go. A quick Google search determined that white wine pairs well, so I threw some in for good measure. The result: dinner for two in under 20 minutes! You could even make this for date night if you're classy like that. I myself avoid cooking for men for stereotypical reasons, but one could always make exceptions.

Dinner for Two: Mini Ravioli w/ Kaleidoscope Chard in White Wine Sauce 

  • 1 T salted butter (I like cooking with the salted stuff but feel free to use unsalted and season to taste)
  • 1 cups dry mini ravioli (Trader Joe's, find in the dried pasta section)
  • 1 bag (6 cups?) prewashed and cut Kalediscope Chard (Trader Joe's)
  • 1 onion
  • 1/2 cup white wine (<= the quality of whatever you're drinking)

1. Make pasta: bring a large pot of water to a boil, and pour in ravioli. Cook for 15-18 min, or until al-dente.
2. MEANWHILE: Heat up a large frying pan over medium-high heat.
3. Finely chop onion (and chard if not pre-cut). When pan is hot, throw in a pat of butter, and onions. Cook until onions are browned and wilted.
4. Add Swiss Chard and pour in the wine. Cook over medium heat until leaves are tender and wine has evaporated.
5. When pasta is ready, drain and add to frying pan. Toss with veggies and sauce, and serve.
