Sweet Potato Fudge Brownies
adapted from neverhomemaker

4 tablespoons canola oil
2/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
1/2 cup all purpose flour (whole wheat pastry flour would be great, and healthier too!)
1/4 teaspoon baking powder
1/4 teaspoon salt
2/3 cup organic cane sugar
2/3 cup sweet-potato puree
1 flax egg (see directions below)
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/2 cup chocolate chips

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees F, and grease an 8" baking pan.
  2. In a small microwave-safe bowl, whisk together 1 tablespoon golden flax meal with three tablespoons water. Then heat on high for 30 seconds. Stir and set aside to cool.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  4. In another bowl, combine the sweet potato puree with the oil, flax egg, vanilla, and sugar. Mix until smooth.
  5. Add the wet ingredients to the dry. Mix until everything is well moistened and stir in the chocolate chips. (The dough will be a bit stiff and extremely fudgy!)
  6. Spread into your prepared pan and bake for about 20 min, or until just set. (These brownies are extremely fudgy so a toothpick won't likely come out clean.)
  7. Enjoy warm and gooey, or let cool a bit for easier slicing :)
