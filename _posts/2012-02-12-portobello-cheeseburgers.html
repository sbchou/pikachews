I love mushrooms and have been meaning for the longest time to try my hand at making Portobello burgers!
The results, as suspected, were fantastic--the mushrooms are meaty and juicy and full of flavor from the marinade, a great contrast to the melty cheese.

Portobello Cheese Burgers
adapted from All Recipes
makes 4 burgers

  • 4 portobello mushroom caps
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 (1 ounce) slices pepper jack or other semi-soft cheese
  • 4 whole wheat hamburger buns
  1. Place the mushroom caps, smooth side up, in a shallow dish. In a small bowl, whisk together vinegar, oil, basil, oregano, garlic, salt, and pepper. Pour over the mushrooms. Let stand at room temperature for 15 minutes or so, turning twice.
  2. Preheat a large saucepan on medium-high heat. (Alternatively, you can grill burgers for a more traditional taste!)
  3. Place mushrooms on the pan, reserving marinade for basting.
  4. Grill uncovered for 5 to 8 minutes on each side, or until tender and sauce is reduced, frequently basting by spooning over leftover marinade.
  5. Meanwhile, lightly toast the hamburger buns.
  6. As soon as portobellos are ready, quickly top with a slice of cheese, and sandwich with desired toppings in hamburger bun.
  7. Enjoy warm!
