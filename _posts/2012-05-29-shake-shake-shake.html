Yesterday my dear friend Lyndsey came to visit and we had quite a delicious day!
We are both big fans of art and so we spent the whole afternoon exploring the MOMA. We managed to go through every room on every floor, in just a bit more than 2 hours!

After that we made a beeline for Shake Shack, New York's most popular burger and shake chain. Unfortunately, it's also a tourist favorite, and so the line for burgers was ridiculously long. We settled for sweets instead, since the line for cold food was much shorter.

This is a "Concrete", which according to Shake Shack's menu is "Dense frozen custard blended at high speed with toppings & mix-ins". I got the Shack Attack® :Chocolate custard, fudge sauce, chocolate truffle cookie dough and Mast Brothers Shake Shack blend chocolate, topped with chocolate sprinkles.

Lyndsey got good ol' plain chocolate, a familiar favorite.
We both thought it was more like soft ice cream than a shake. In fact, I couldn't really tell what made it a "concrete". Of course, ice cream is always welcome on a hot, muggy day, but maybe I would just go for the cheaper alternative next time.

Thank goodness Madison Square Eats (a mini food stand fest) was going on across the street! All the food looked amazing, and I choose to try the vegetarian Mel + Steve (asian sesame slaw on top of veggie dog) from AsiaDog, an Asian Fusion hot dog stand.

Lyndsey and I both agreed it was by far the prettiest hot dog (with the purple cabbage slaw) that we'd ever seen! And the taste was great, too :)

Note: Later that night I actually went to Shake Shack AGAIN, for a hackNY team-building event, and got to try the famous 'Shroom Burger (deep fried portabello stuffed with Muenster), with cheese fries on the side. They were to die for! I don't see anything great in the shakes, but the hot food at Shake Shack is greasy goodness.
