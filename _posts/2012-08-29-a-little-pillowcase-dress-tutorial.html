Every time I go home in the 'burbs I'm inevitably bitten by a case of the sewing blog. Starved from craft supplies and sewing machines all year will do that to ya.

However, since I'm rather impatient, and I'm hardly at home for long, I'm always looking for shortcuts and clever refashions. Pinterest is great for ideas like that, where I happened upon this neat trick: making a simple light dress or nightgown out of a pillowcase!

I followed the full instructions given here aside from using an old tank top to trace a pattern for the armholes instead of cutting straight across (I wanted a bit more curve and dimension to the fabric and sharp corners are really tough to hem).

I got these pretty pillowcases from a thrift store for just $1.49 each. So cheap, and it's nice to feel good about recycling :) Altogether, with ribbon and all, the supplies for 4 dresses was not even ten dollars!

The dress I am wearing in the photo started like this.

For a quick tutorial, (the concept is really simple!)
All you need is:
*A pillowcase
*about 2 yards of ribbon between 1-1.5 inches thick
*thread
*safety pin

1. Cut the top off the closed end of the pillowcase.
2. Use an old tank top to trace out armholes on either side, lining it up with the cut end. (It should go about 8 inches down from the top if you are freehanding.)
3. Hem armholes
4. Hem both sides of cut seam, then fold each about 1.5 inches down if using 1 inch ribbon, else about 2 inches. Sew down this fold.
5. Clip safety pin onto one end of ribbon and thread through casting on both sides. Tie ends into a nice bow.
6. Slip over head and wear as a flowy nightgown, or belt for a daytime dress!

Enjoy!
