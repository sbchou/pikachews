It's the most Wonderful Time of the Year!

No matter what holiday(s) you celebrate or don't celebrate, I think it can be generally agreed upon that (insert-denomination-or-non-spiritual-belief-here) cookies sure are nice.

This recipe is especially good-- I stumbled upon it from Taste of Home magazine. Of course, since I can never leave anything be, I adapted it from the original pinwheels to more Christmas-appropriate cut-outs (any excuse to use my Miffy cutters from Tokyo!).

The dough is very simple-- just flour, sugar, butter, eggs, and a touch of vanilla, so make sure to use top-quality (organic) butter here-- the flavor really shines. And it smells heavenly baking!

Best Butter Cookies
Yield: ~3 dozen
Adapted from these pinwheels

Ingredients:
* 1/2 c butter
* 1 c sugar
* 1 egg
* 1 t vanilla (almond)
* 2 c AP flour
* 1 t baking powder
* 1 pinch salt

Directions:
1. In a large bowl, cream butter & sugar till light & fluffy. Beat in egg and vanilla.
3. In another bowl, mix flour & baking powder. Gradually add to creamed mixture and mix well (it will be a bit stiff but will come together!).
4. Divide dough into 2 portions, plastic wrap, and chill for 2 hours or till firm. If planning on slice & bakes, roll into 2 logs.
5. When ready to cook, preheat oven to 350F and line 2 baking sheets.
6. Either slice dough into 1/8" thick rounds or roll 1/8" thick and cut with cookie cutters. Place 1/2" apart on sheet (won't spread much) and bake ~10 min, or till edges are lightly browned.
7. Cool completely before icing.
