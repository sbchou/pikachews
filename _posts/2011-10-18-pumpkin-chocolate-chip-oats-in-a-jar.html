Have you heard of the desserts-in-a-jar trend lately?

I think it's such a adorable and clever idea!
Although nothing can beat the timelessness of a good old slab of chocolate layer cake, there's just something so charming and kitschy about glass jars. I'm quite a fan of them.

Spicy, warm, and comforting, this simple recipe is the perfect fall day treat. Stir it up in an old peanut butter jar like I did and you have a healthy and delicious breakfast (or dessert!) on the go.

Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Oats in a Jar
Makes 1 serving
-1/2 c instant oatmeal
-1/2-1 c boiling water
-1/4 c pumpkin puree (use the good stuff! Organic tastes better :) )
-splash of milk or soy milk
-dash of cinnamon
-handful of chocolate chips

Put the oatmeal in your jar or bowl, and pour boiling water over it (the amount depends on how thick you like it--I like mine nice and gooey). Now here's the hard part: let it sit for at least 10 minutes. The longer you let it sit, the thicker it will be. You could use the microwave or even a saucepan if you are impatient like me or you want to use old fashioned or steel cut oats, both of which are delicious.
Once your oats are nice and thick, stir in the the pumpkin, a splash of milk, and the cinnamon.
Sprinkle chocolate chips, give it a good stir, and savor with a cup of tea.
