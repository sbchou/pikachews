How is it possible to have so many hipsters per square foot? Where did they all come from? What the heck is Williamsburg?

These are the questions I ask myself as I sit here sipping my iced coffee, munching on a strawberry cream cheese carrot muffin, and mooching off free wifi on this hot hot day. My blouse, pearl earrings and sensible work flats stick out like a sore thumb amidst this tattooed, bespectacled, granny-dress-wearing crowd. Will I succumb to the plethora of vintage stores? Will I start drinking kombucha and eating gluten free pizza? Only time will tell.

But for now, we will always have coffee, and goddam, is Williamsburg full of great cafes.

THE LOW DOWN:

Variety Cafe

368 Graham Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11211

Pros: Stumptown beans, plenty of outlets, free wifi

Odds of getting a table: high, compared to manhattan; low, compared to suburbia

Taste test: I had a regular iced coffee and poured in a little whole milk. Flavor was rich, nutty and delicious. Yay Stumptown! Bonus for muffin selection.

Recommended: yes!
