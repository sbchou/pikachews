It's been quite a hellish week for me at school...

But here are Thanksgiving photos, finally!

Thanksgiving is one of my favorite holidays. Not only does it mark the beginning of the holiday season, it comes at a usually stressful time in the semester where taking a weekend off and putting it all in perspective  with the ones I love most is much needed.

And did I mention the food?

This was our fancy menu, planned out by my Aunt Joy, who every year hosts our holiday dinner and comes up with a delicious and creative theme!

This year, we went all-out Italian. The results were impressive, considering that none of us are the slightest bit Italian nor have had any experience with the cuisine!

First up:
For lunch, a delicious Cioppino ( a hearty seafood stew full of shrimp, mussels, clam, crab, and scallops!) with homemade bread spiced with rosemary (courtesy of Uncle Ken).

After filling ourselves to the brim with seafood, it was time to start cooking for the main event--Thanksgiving dinner!

This year, instead of the traditional turkey, we decided to throw a curveball by making roast pork instead--slow roasted in a dutch oven all day until the meat was tender and delicious.

 

Here's a glass of sparkling pomegranate juice, which was a lovely and light non-alcoholic alternative.

A light salad of arugula, raddichio, and romaine with aged balsamic dressing

One of my favorites: roasted root vegetables with thyme and oregano!

I absolutely adore roasted veggies, and this combination of parsnips, rutabaga, turnips, and sweet potato was superb.

(I'm still enjoying the leftovers at school!)

Peas studded with bits of fried pancetta and pine nuts was a sweet-and-salty delight.

Here's the roast pork, carved for the dinner table. It was so tender that it fell apart at the touch!

The set table

My dinner plate (well, the first plate, anyway)

And just when you thought it couldn't get any better, dessert time!
My aunt and uncle made an impressive "Crostata di Mele con Frangipane"--apple tart with homemade frangipane!
It was so beautiful and rich.

We topped it off with vanilla gelato!

After that...a nap!

It was so much fun to prepare and eat such an elaborate feast.

Something about being away at college and living on my own definitely makes me appreciate a nice warm homemade meal that much more.
