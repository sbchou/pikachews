Peanut butter cookies.

Two kinds.

Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Oatmeal Cookies

Makes 1 dozen
adapted from Handle the Heat
With whole wheat flour, oatmeal, and peanut butter, these are cookies are wholesome yet seriously addictive!
They have great texture: crispy outside, soft melty inside, full of chocolate chips & pb.
  • ½ c pb
  • ½ c brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ c whole wheat flour
  • ¼ c oatmeal
  • ¼ t cinnamon
  • ½ t baking soda
  • ¼ c chocolate chips
  1. Preheat oven to 350F and grease a baking sheet.
  2. In a large bowl, mix pb and brown sugar until smooth. Beat in egg.
  3. Stir in flour, then mix in oats, baking soda, and cinnamon. Fold in chocolate chips. Don’t overmix!
  4. Scoop out tablespoons of dough, place on baking sheet, and flatten slightly.
  5. Bake for 10-12 min, or until tops are lightly golden.
  6. Cool on a wire rack. Cookies may be stored in an airtight container for 3-4 days...if they last!

Peanut Butter-Nutella Thumbprints
Makes about 15 cookies
Adapted from That’s So Vegan
Nutella and peanut butter: one dream team. These cookies are sooo good, with a melt-in-mouth honey-scented dough and rich filling. I made them for my school newspaper's Secret Santa and they were quite well received!
  • 1 c AP flour
  • ½ t baking soda
  • ½ t salt
  • ½ c pb
  • ½ c honey
  • ⅙ c oil
  • 1 t vanilla
  • Nutella for filling (about ½ c)
  1. Preheat oven to 350F and line 2 cookie sheets.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, stir together pb, honey, oil, and vanilla until creamy. Stir in dry ingredients until combined.
  4. Roll tbs of dough into balls and place on baking sheets.
  5. Bake for about 8-10 min, or until lightly browned.
  6. Make indents with thumb, and fill with Nutella!
  7. Cool completely before serving.
