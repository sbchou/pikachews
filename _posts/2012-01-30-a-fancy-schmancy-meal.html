Oh Trader Joe's, how I love you.
I love you even when your line wraps all the way around to the milk aisle and you make my weekly grocery bill double.
How could I not be inspired to whip up something special?

Lemon Pepper Mahi Mahi with Balsamic Baby Zucchini & Whole Wheat Couscous

This was an ad-lib sort of meal that went something like this:

1. Get thee a bag of adorable little baby zucchini. Try not to feel too bad about eating them all about.

2. Slice them in half lengthwise (ignore the screaming). Sautee with olive oil, a good sprinkling of salt & pepper, & douse with Lemon Pepper Mrs. Dash seasoning. When nice and browned, drizzle on some good balsamic vinegar and cook until reduced. (I might omit the vinegar next time: it was yummy but the flavor of the zucchini was so delicate some of it was overwhelmed.)

3. Meanwhile, defrost your frozen Mahi Mahi steaks under cold running water.

4. When it is all nice and defrosted open up the package and curse the manufacturing for putting the cooking instructions insideÂ the wrapping so you had to google "how to cook frozen mahi mahi" for 15 minutes.

5. Sautee for about 3 min a side in a pan drizzled with olive oil, and sprinkled with salt, pepper, and once again, lemon pepper seasoning (poor man's lemons?), until fish is opaque but not dry.

6. Win friends and influence people OR shut door and enjoy all to yourself. (I choose the latter.)

(Sorry for the lack of real recipe: just be creative and it will be delicious, I promise! Voila the magic of Trader Joe's.)
