To spread the joy, here's a recipe for a super fun party cake that I made for an LGBT Hacking meetup last night.

The cake was a snap to make (seriously, 3 ingredients!) and so pretty.

The recipe uses a neat trick (diet cream soda instead of eggs and oil) which turned out perfectly light and fluffy.

Who would have guessed?

Rainbow Swirly Cake

inspired by http://www.omnomicon.com/rainbowcake

makes 1 13x9” cake

  • 1 box white (vanilla) cake mix
  • 1 12 oz can diet cream soda
  • 1 pack (4 or more colors) food dye
  1. Preheat oven to 350F and grease the cake pan.
  2. In a large bowl, dump cake mix and pour in soda.
  3. Stir until no lumps remain (for the most part).
  4. Now the fun part:
  5. Divide batter among 4 small bowls. In each bowl, add many (1 tsp) drops of food dye. The original recipe called for gel coloring, which is more intense, but the colors turn out just fine provided you put a lot of coloring in.
  6. Pour all of the first color into the pan, and spread it out.
  7. Now, pour the other color on top and overlapping.
  8. Pour the other color in another corner, and the last one in the other.
  9. Basically, you want large blocks of color.
  10. Using a knife, gently swirl 1-2 times to create a tie-dye effect. Do not over-swirl or else you will get a muddy mess!
  11. Bake approx. 20 minutes (a little less than the box instructions-- don’t let it overbake!).
  12. Cool before frosting with funfetti, of course!
