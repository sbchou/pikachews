Last summer I interned at the online gaming company, OMGPOP. It was a great experience--when else do you get to play games on the job?? Not to mention the awesome co-workers!

Today's recipe is inspired by one of my favorite games on the site: Dinglepop!

They're mini homemade funfetti cupcakes decorated to look like the dingles. It's a lot simpler than it looks, and a great treat for any gamer friends you may have.

Dinglepopcakes Tutorial

Makes about 48

Supplies:

  • 1 batch of your favorite white cake recipe (you can use boxed cake mix--I'm just a from-scratch sort of girl :) )
  • 1 recipe your favorite vanilla frosting (again, a can of vanilla frosting will do the trick!)
  • red, blue, and yellow food coloring
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips
  • a mini-muffin tin
  • mini-cupcake liners
Directions:
Mix up your cake batter according to the recipe or boxed directions. For fun, I threw in a handful of rainbow sprinkles, for instant funfetti!

Scoop into lined muffin cups, and bake for about 10-12 minutes at 350 degrees. Let them cool completely on wire racks.

Meanwhile, make the frosting!

Separate into five bowls, and stir in yellow food coloring into one, blue in another, and a combination ofÂ red + blue in the third until you get purple, and the same for green (blue + yellow) and orange (yellow + red). The toughest part of this whole project was getting the colors right--food coloring tends to look a bit muted once you stir it into frosting, and in the end my colors weren't exactly like those in the game, but don't worry, it will be tasty regardless!
For simplicity's sake, I only made three colors of dingles, but since you're less of a slacker than I am, you should make all five :)

Now, frost away!

Once you're all done frosting (whew!) melt the chocolate chips in a small microwavable bowl in the microwave for about 30 -60 seconds, stirring until the chocolate's melted smooth.

Now for the fun part: using a toothpick, draw two small lines on each cupcake for "eyes".

Leave your cupcakes be and play a few games of Dinglepop so that the chocolate will harden and set.

Feel free to arrange them into fun gameplays!
