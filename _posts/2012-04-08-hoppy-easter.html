I first spied this adorable concept of a cupcake in, of course, Martha Stewart magazine. (Although, now that I look at it, my chicks turned out a bit different in style, but no less cute, n'est-ce pas? Martha seemed to have iced the cupcake upside down for a nice tapered shape, that clever bastard.)

Anyhoo, this proved to be quite a fun & uplifting Sunday afternoon project.
Here's a rough sketch/recipe:

Baby Chick Cupcakes
Makes 12
Adapted from Martha Stewart Living

Ingredients:

  • 1 recipe for 1 dozen of your favorite vanilla cupcakes (you can use cake mix, too!)
  • 1 can vanilla frosting (I used the "whipped" kind to make it nice and fluffy)
  • about 1/2 bag coconut, unsweetened
  • mini chocolate chips (24)
  • toasted whole almonds (36)
  1. Prepare cupcakes and bake according to instructions. Let cool completely.
  2. Meanwhile, toast the coconut: spread into a baking sheet and bake at 350F for about 10 min, stirring constantly. Be careful not to let it burn. Let cool completely.
  3. When everything's nice and cooled, it's time to assemble the chicks!
  4. Frost the bottom andÂ sides of each cupcake and coat thoroughly/roll in coconut. (Note: I actually messed up and frosted the top and sides, hence my slightly squashed-looking chicks. Still yummy, though!)
  5. For each chick, stick on 2 chocolate chips and an almond for the beak. Push two almond into the bottom for feet.
  6. Repeat until you have a nice little coop of chickies :)
  7. Eat, if you can bring yourself to do it!
