Nothing smells spells weekend like pancakes!
Unlike this recipe for single-serving Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Pancakes, (which are fluffy and delicious and pumpkin-y and chocolate-y but also makes a wee bit too much pancake for one petite person), these Choco-Banana recipe makes just 2 pancakes: perfect for a sweet breakfast treat: no more, no less.

Choco-Banana Pancakes for One

Serves 1! (Makes 2 pancakes)

  • ¼ c whole wheat flour
  • ½ t baking powder
  • ½ T sugar
  • a dash of cinnamon
  • a pinch of salt
  • ¼ c soy or almond milk
  • ½ t canola oil
  • a handful of chocolate chips
  • 1/2 banana, sliced
  1. Lightly grease a skillet and preheat over medium heat.
  2. Meanwhile, stir together all dry ingredients in a bowl, and all the wet in another. Add the wet into the dry and stir just to combine. Stir in chocolate chips.
  3. Ladle about ¼ cups of batter onto preheated skillet and top each pancake with a few slices of banana. Cook for 2-3 min. or until bubbles form along the top and edges are firm. Flip and cook for another 2-3 min.
  4. Serve warm.
